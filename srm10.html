1. Sutton, D. (2021) ‚Äì Information Risk Management: A Practitioner's Guide (2nd ed.)

Key Points:

  • Information Risk Management (IRM): A way to identify, assess, and reduce risks to information in an organisation.
  • Alignment with Goals: IRM should support the organisation‚Äôs overall objectives.
  • Risk Assessment: Methods to examine threats, weaknesses, and potential impacts.
  • Controls: Steps to lower risks to an acceptable level.
  • Continuous Improvement: Regularly review and update risk processes as threats and systems change.

Summary: Sutton (2021) explains how organisations can protect their information through structured risk management. The book shows practical ways to assess risks, apply controls, and link risk management to business goals. It also stresses reviewing and updating processes regularly to address new challenges.

Reference: Sutton, D. (2021) Information Risk Management: A Practitioner's Guide. 2nd edn. BCS, The Chartered Institute for IT.

2. Popov, G., Lyon, B.K. and Hollcroft, B.D. (2016) ‚Äì Risk Assessment: A Practical Guide to Assessing Operational Risks

Key Points:

  • Risk Identification: Spot potential risks in operations.
  • Risk Analysis: Examine how likely risks are and how serious their impact might be.
  • Risk Evaluation: Decide which risks are most important to address.
  • Risk Treatment: Choose to reduce, accept, avoid, or transfer risks.
  • Monitoring: Keep track of risks and update actions as needed.

Summary: Popov, Lyon, and Hollcroft (2016) guide readers through assessing and managing operational risks. They explain simple steps to identify, analyse, and deal with risks in everyday business. The book helps organisations handle threats in a structured, practical way.

Reference: Popov, G., Lyon, B.K. and Hollcroft, B.D. (2016) Risk Assessment: A Practical Guide to Assessing Operational Risks. Hoboken, NJ: Wiley.
